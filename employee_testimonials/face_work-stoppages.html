The Former And Current Employees (FACE) of Amazon >

Work stoppages

After reading the accounts on your web site and looking at the behavior at my own work site, it's clear that Amazon has a systemic problem with the way it's run.

They hire people fresh out of college with no experience and put them in Level 4 or Level 5 positions, while the part-timers who do all the work can't qualify for those positions because they'd have to be there at least two years before they could possibly apply, assuming one opens up. So those newbie college graduates and veterans they hire as AMs have the connections to people higher up who are in charge of promoting them, while the part-timers can't get those connections.

It's no wonder that when people get close to their two-year period for employment there that management starts coming up with all sorts of "failures" by those associates, which lead to write-ups, which then lead to people becoming so frustrated with working there that they quit; or for those that won't give them that satisfaction, terminated.

Amazing, but management never seems to understand that their behavior is what causes people to quit. HR never finds anything wrong with them, or even HR itself; we currently have an interim HRBP who pretends that she's there for us, but in reality she's not; she's only there to find fault with others so she can terminate them. She doesn't understand that she's not following the Amazon standards of conduct in how she talks to people, so she's in no legitimate position to criticize others.

Let's face it: Amazon treats their employees shabbily, just like Trump treats people, which is why nobody wants to work for him. It's time we all send a clear message to Amazon HQ that we're mad as Hell and we aren't going to take it anymore. We should stage work stoppages at every FC and SC for days until HQ comes to the bargaining table and meets our demands for better pay, better treatment, accountability for management abuses (including HR), and real promotional opportunities for us all.

They can't fire you for coordinated activities.
